The PlayStation 5 is on the brink of a significant jailbreak, but it hinges on acquiring a rare physical game. According to reports, hackers are eyeing the limited edition of "Star Wars: Racer Revenge" for the PlayStation 4, a game that has become a hot commodity due to its scarcity. With only 8,500 copies released by Limited Run Games, the race to secure this title is intensifying as prices soar.

This potential vulnerability mirrors past incidents with the PlayStation 3 and Nintendo Switch, where both consoles faced fundamental compromises, leading to the creation of new hardware. The implications of a successful PS5 jailbreak could reshape the gaming landscape, offering players unprecedented access but also raising concerns about security and piracy.
