Stephen's Sausage Roll, released in 2016, has not only captivated players with its unique mechanics but has also significantly influenced the landscape of puzzle game design. Often compared to classics like Sokoban, this game has introduced a new genre of puzzles that developers now refer to as 'sausage-like,' showcasing its lasting impact on the gaming community.

Set on an intriguing island, players navigate a character armed with a fork and a sausage, pushing the sausage onto barbecue grills to cook it. The challenge lies in the game's mechanics, where players must think critically about movement and strategy to avoid pitfalls like dropping the sausage into the sea or cooking the same side twice. With an impressive Metascore of 90 and a thriving community, it's no wonder that Thinky Games hailed it as one of the most influential puzzle games of all time.

As we look to the future of puzzle games, Stephen's Sausage Roll serves as a benchmark for innovation and creativity. How will upcoming developers build on this legacy to create the next generation of engaging puzzles?
